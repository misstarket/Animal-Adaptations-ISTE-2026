How animals survive — and how they might evolve.

A 5th-grade science project where students researched real animal adaptations, then used AI as a thinking partner to predict how their animal might evolve millions of years from now.

AI-Ready Graduate Roles Practiced
The Process

From field notes to future evolution

Part 1 — Their Animal Today
Specimen 01

Meet My Animal

Common & scientific name, classification, size, diet, and three cool facts — plus a photo or drawing.

Specimen 02

Its Environment

Biome, climate, location, food web, and the dangers or predators it faces day to day.

Specimen 03

Its Adaptations Right Now

3–5 current adaptations, each tagged structural, behavioral, or physiological, with how it helps survival.

Part 2 — Their Animal's Future
Specimen 04

A Changing Environment

Students choose a brand-new biome, or describe how their animal's home has changed due to humans.

Specimen 05

Future Adaptations

3–5 predicted adaptations the animal might evolve over millions of years to survive its new reality.

Specimen 06

Wrap-Up Reflection

What surprised them, whether the animal could really survive, and how human impact shapes adaptation.

Where AI came in

Predicting a future adaptation is creative but falsifiable — exactly the kind of task AI is good at supporting and bad at doing unsupervised. Students used AI to stress-test their predictions against real evolutionary logic, catching moments where the AI got biology wrong and correcting it themselves.
